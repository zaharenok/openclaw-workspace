## Partnership Strategy (Bioprom-Center, 2015-2016)

I took over as Chairman when revenue was €8M annually. Eighteen months later: €35M.

Here's what actually happened.

We sold veterinary supplies. Fifty distributors competed in Russia. Most tried to undercut each other on price. Race to the bottom.

I thought: what if we stopped competing and started partnering?

Went to the top 20 distributors by market share. Offered them exclusive supply terms if they made us their primary vendor. Volume discounts. Extended payment terms. Marketing support.

Eighty-seven distributors eventually joined.

Built them a portal where they could check inventory and place orders automatically. Ran joint webinars. Co-branded whitepapers on veterinary trends.

Revenue didn't grow linearly — it exploded. €8M to €35M in 18 months. Market share went from 12% to 31%.

Customer acquisition cost dropped 65% because distributors brought us customers for free. Repeat purchase rate hit 68% (industry average: 42%).

The insight: don't build sales channels. Build partner ecosystems. Your partners become your growth team.

## Onboarding Fix (Bioprom-Impex, 2014-2017)

We had a churn problem.

New customers would sign up, place one order, then disappear. Analytics showed 43% churned before the second purchase. They just didn't know what to do with our product catalog.

So we redesigned onboarding.

Gave every new customer a dedicated person who walked them through their first order. Sent a free product sample kit (cost us €45, created urgency). Offered a 30-day return policy if they weren't happy.

Then we automated follow-ups:
- Day 7: Check-in email with catalog tips
- Day 14: Usage analytics + personalized recommendations
- Day 30: Discount on next order

Took 90 days to design, a year to fully roll out.

Results: activation rate jumped from 34% to 67%. Ninety-day retention went from 41% to 73%. Time to first purchase dropped from 18 days to 9. Lifetime value increased 2.3x.

The lesson: get users to their first "aha moment" fast. Everything else follows.
